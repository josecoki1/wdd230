Hick's Law

IKEA

Ikea.com

The idea behind Hick's Law is increasing response time from the user by having the least number of decisions available. In this example we see how Ikea has shortened their selection for a search for Kitchen items by creating sub-categories that the user can pick from. Hence, choosing from 3 sub-categories is faster and easier than choosing from a list of 100+ items.

Visual Hierarchy

CNN

CNN.com

In Visual Hierarchy, the principle is to use design elements to make items stand out based on their order of importance. In a news site, like CNN, the editors choose those articles that are either of more importance, trending, or that will keep people more interested in their site by placing those at the top of the site, adding size and weight changes on the fonts, as well as including pictures relevant to their articles. PARC: Alignment

Family Search

familysearch.com

The principle of alignment should encourage us to integrate visual and text elements without it appearing to having those elements appear random. There should be a visual connection between elements without causing the user to be confused on then placement or connection between them.
